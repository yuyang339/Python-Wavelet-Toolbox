DiscreteWavelets Toolbox

IBWT1D1

One-dimensional biorthogonal inverse wavelet transformation, one iteration

Syntax

	v = IBWT1D1(wt,h,ht)

	v = IBWT1D1(wt,h,ht,options)

Description

v = IBWT1D1(wt,h,ht) returns one iteration of the one-dimensional biorthogonal inverse wavelet transformation of wt using symmetric biorthogonal filters h and ht.

v = IBWT1D1(wt,h,ht,'Boundary','Reflective') returns one iteration of the one-dimensional biorthogonal inverse wavelet transformation of v using symmetric biorthogonal filters h and ht, but exploits the fact that the filters are symmetric to produce a transformation that better handles edge effects. See Section 11.3 in the text for more details.

Remarks

The length of wt must be an even integer.

You must supply the routine with a symmetric pair of biorthogonal filters. That is, in the case when the lengths of both h and ht are odd, then the filters must be symmetric about h0 and ht0. In the case when the lengths are even, then the filters should satisfy hk = h1-k and htk = ht1-k.

Example

Here we compute one iteration of the one-dimensional biorthogonal wavelet transformation and then use IBWT1D1 to obtain the original vector. 

v=1:32; %Create a vector
[h,ht]=SplineFilters(2,2); %Load the (5,3) spline filter pair
wt=BWT1D1(v,h,ht) %Compute the transformation
y=IBWT1D1(wt,h,ht) %Compute the inverse transformation
abs(v'-y) %Compare with original vector v

Here is the same example but utilizes the boundary conditions (see Section 11.3 of the text). 

v=1:32; %Create a vector
[h,ht]=SplineFilters(2,2); %Load the (5,3) spline filter pair
wt=BWT1D1(v,h,ht,'Boundary','Reflective') %Compute the transformation
y=IBWT1D1(wt,h,ht,'Boundary','Reflective') %Compute the inverse transformation
abs(v'-y) %Compare with original vector v
